# Dan Harmon's Story Structure Series (101–106)

## Summary
A six-part tutorial series by [[dan-harmon]] on the fundamentals of story structure, originally published on the Channel 101 wiki. The series introduces the [[harmon-story-circle]] — an 8-step circular narrative model — and progressively elaborates on it from basic pattern recognition through theoretical grounding, simplification, detailed application, television-specific adaptation, and practical 5-minute pilot writing.

## Content

### Part 101 — Super Basic Shit
The simplest presentation of the 8-step circle: a character in a zone of comfort wants something, enters an unfamiliar situation, adapts, gets what they wanted, pays a price, returns, and has changed. Harmon presents this as an instinctive, universal pattern found in movies, dreams, fairy tales, and music.

### Part 102 — Pure, Boring Theory
Grounds the circular model in three nested rhythms: the rhythm of biology (life/death/evolution), the rhythm of psychology (conscious/unconscious mind), and the rhythm of society (order/chaos). Stories resonate because they mirror these fundamental rhythms.

### Part 103 — Let's Simplify Before Moving On
Distills the 8 steps to their primal essence: You → Need → Go → Search → Find → Take → Return → Change. This "caveman" version reflects the ancient survival imperative that drives all human storytelling.

### Part 104 — The Juicy Details
Maps each of the 8 steps to Joseph Campbell's [[monomyth]] / Hero's Journey terminology, providing detailed writing advice for each step with examples from Die Hard, The Matrix, Network, and other films.

### Part 105 — How TV is Different
Explains how television modifies the circle: where films emphasize step 8 (change), TV emphasizes returning to the status quo to keep the series running. The TV pilot covers steps 1–3, inviting the audience to watch step 4 forever.

### Part 106 — Five Minute Pilots
Applies the model to [[channel-101]]'s 5-minute pilot format, breaking down time allocations: 37.5 seconds per step, 75 seconds per quarter. Includes an FAQ addressing common objections to the model.

## Key Takeaways
- The [[harmon-story-circle]] is an 8-step circular pattern: You, Need, Go, Search, Find, Take, Return, Change
- The model is grounded in biological, psychological, and social rhythms (article 102)
- Harmon maps his steps to Campbell's [[monomyth]]: Call to Adventure, Crossing the Threshold, Road of Trials, Meeting with the Goddess, Atonement with the Father, Magic Flight, Master of Both Worlds
- Television stories emphasize return-to-status-quo rather than transformative change (article 105)
- For 5-minute pilots, each step gets approximately 37.5 seconds (article 106)
