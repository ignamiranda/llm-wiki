# AI Speciation

## Definition

The hypothesis that AI models will diversify into specialized niches rather than remaining a monoculture of general-purpose models. Analogous to the animal kingdom where brains are specialized for different ecological niches (overdeveloped visual cortex in predators, etc.), AI models will speciate into domain-specific variants optimized for mathematics, code, creative writing, or other tasks. This contrasts with the current trend of frontier labs attempting to build a single model that is arbitrarily intelligent across all domains.

## Key Properties

- Specialized models can be smaller and more efficient than general ones
- Speciation enables lower latency and higher throughput for specific tasks
- Currently limited by undeveloped science of fine-tuning without capability loss
- MoE (mixture of experts) is a partial step but opaque to users
- Open-source models naturally fill niches via fine-tuning
- The "jaggedness" of AI capabilities motivates speciation
