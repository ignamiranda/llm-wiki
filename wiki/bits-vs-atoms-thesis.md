# Bits vs Atoms Thesis

## Definition

Andrej Karpathy's thesis that AI's impact will manifest first and fastest in the digital domain because manipulating bits is fundamentally easier than manipulating atoms. Digital information can be copy-pasted, modified, and processed at near-zero marginal cost. Physical manipulation requires energy, materials, and time. The sequence of AI-driven transformation is: digital first → digital-physical interfaces → pure physical.

## Key Properties

- Digital: "speed of light" transformation
- Physical: lags behind by years
- There is an "overhang" of already-digital information waiting to be processed
- Digital-physical interfaces (sensors, actuators) are the next frontier
- Physical market may be larger but arrives later
- Information markets will emerge to feed AI systems sensor data

## Examples

Legal document analysis, code generation, and digital design will transform far faster than warehouse robotics, construction, or manufacturing. Sensor data markets (photos from Tehran, lab experiments) are emerging to bridge digital AI with physical world data.
