# Emotional Procedural Generation

## Definition

Jill Murray's approach to procedural generation prioritizes genuine emotional connection over structural innovation. By providing sparse character detail, the system provokes players to project their own experiences and feelings into the gaps, creating stronger emotional investment than highly detailed generation could achieve.

## Key Properties

- **Sparse character generation** — 10 carefully chosen fields create more emotional resonance than 50 exhaustive fields
- **Player projection fills gaps** — Players naturally fill in missing details with their own experiences, creating personal connection
- **Characters as anchors** — Procedurally generated characters serve as emotional touchpoints for the player
- **Comedy is easier than drama** — Procedural systems can produce comedy more reliably than genuine dramatic moments
- **Innovation is next to procrastination** — Chasing novel procedural techniques can be a form of avoiding the hard work of emotional design

## Examples

Murray discusses how procedurally generated characters in narrative games can create emotional attachment when they have just enough detail to feel real but enough gaps for player projection to operate.
