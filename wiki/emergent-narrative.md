# Emergent Narrative

## Definition

Emergent narrative refers to stories that arise spontaneously from the interaction of game simulation systems and player actions, without being explicitly authored. Unlike traditional authored narratives (pre-written plot) or procedurally generated plot (algorithmically assembled from authored fragments), emergent narrative is a property of deep simulation: the player's experience of cause-and-effect chains, unexpected interactions, and character-driven events that the designer did not explicitly script or anticipate.

## Key Properties

- Bottom-up — stories emerge from system rules, not top-down plot design
- Player-driven — the player's choices and interpretations shape the narrative
- Unpredictable — the designer cannot predict all possible narrative outcomes
- Requires depth — shallow systems produce trivial emergence; deep, interconnected systems produce rich narratives
- Interpretation-dependent — emergent events become "stories" when players assign narrative meaning to them

## Examples

- **Dwarf Fortress** (Tarn Adams) — the canonical example: individual dwarfs with personalities, histories, and relationships generate complex narratives of love, loss, madness, and catastrophe through deep simulation
- **The Sims** — dramatic play patterns emerge from character needs, relationships, and unexpected interactions
- **Caves of Qud** — procedurally generated history and faction dynamics create emergent world narratives
- **RimWorld** — AI storyteller system orchestrates events that create emergent colony narratives
