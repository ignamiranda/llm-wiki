# Locomotion System at GDC 2009!

## Summary

Rune's GDC 2009 session proposal "Dynamic Walking with Semi-Procedural Animation" was accepted. An updated interactive demo of the Locomotion System was released featuring a coyote character, dynamic terrain controls, gamepad support, and visualization options.

## Content

The GDC 2009 session "Dynamic Walking with Semi-Procedural Animation" was officially accepted, marking a milestone for the [[procedural-animation-locomotion-system]] project. To accompany the talk, an updated interactive demo was released featuring a coyote character navigating dynamically through varied terrain.

The demo included controls for adjusting stairs, slopes, and uneven ground in real time, along with full gamepad support. Visualization modes were added to help demonstrate the underlying semi-procedural animation techniques. Players could watch the coyote adapt its gait to terrain changes on the fly.

The post also noted Rune's move to Copenhagen and the start of his work at Unity Technologies, connecting the locomotion research to the broader game development industry.

## Key Takeaways

- GDC 2009 lecture on semi-procedural animation was accepted
- Interactive demo with dynamic terrain adaptation using a coyote character
- Moved to Copenhagen, began work at Unity Technologies
