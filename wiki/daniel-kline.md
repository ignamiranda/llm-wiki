# Daniel Kline

## Bio

Daniel Kline is a game designer and researcher who developed the framework for "dramatic gameplay" — four fundamentals for role-playing in games: take on a role, make it personal, perform it, and the game recognizes it. His work analyzes The Sims as the primary example of dramatic play, introducing concepts like fail forward design and dramatic currency. He authored Chapter 10 "Dramatic Play in The Sims."

## Key Contributions

- Developed the four-fundamental framework for dramatic gameplay in role-playing
- Introduced concepts of fail forward design and dramatic currency
- Analyzed The Sims as the primary case study for dramatic play
- Authored Chapter 10 "Dramatic Play in The Sims"
