# Froth

## Definition

Froth is a LARP (Live Action Role-Playing) term for the post-game storytelling phase where players excitedly recount and narrativize what happened during play. Adam Saltsman argues that froth is the most important part of gaming — the social experience of sharing stories — and that games should be designed to maximize the quality of post-game storytelling rather than just the in-game experience.

## Key Properties

- Post-game social phase where players narrate their experiences
- The stories told in froth are often more compelling than what actually happened in the game
- Game design should optimize for what players will say about the experience, not just what they experience
- Froth is inherently collaborative and emerges from player communities
- Related to the plot vs. story distinction: games generate plots, but froth is where players generate stories

## Examples

LARP participants after a session sharing dramatic moments around a campfire. Players of Overland recounting emergent survival stories to friends after a play session. The social experience of discussing emergent narrative moments from games like Dwarf Fortress or Caves of Qud.
