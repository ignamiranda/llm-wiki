# Charts to Visualize How Much You Owe Unity

## Summary

Unity's announced per-install Runtime Fee was analyzed with worked charts demonstrating that for premium games, the unbounded install-based fees could exceed gross revenue. The industry backlash led Unity to backtrack and cap the fee.

## Content

In September 2023, Unity announced a Runtime Fee that would charge developers per game installation after certain thresholds. Charts were created to visualize the financial impact across different game types — premium titles, free-to-play, and subscription models. The analysis showed that for premium games with a fixed purchase price, the per-install fee could quickly exceed the revenue earned per copy, creating an unsustainable and unpredictable cost structure.

The announcement triggered widespread backlash from the game development community. Unity eventually backtracked on the original proposal and introduced a capped fee structure. The episode highlighted the tensions between game engine providers and developers over monetization models.

## Key Takeaways

- Unity's per-install fee could exceed gross revenue for premium games
- Unity eventually backtracked and capped the fee
