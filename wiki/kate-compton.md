# Dr. Kate Compton

## Bio

Dr. Kate Compton is an AI researcher who created Tracery, a grammar-based generation library widely used for Twitterbots and procedural text generation. She identified the "10,000 Bowls of Oatmeal Problem" — the insight that mathematical uniqueness does not equal perceptual uniqueness — and developed the generative methods taxonomy, which categorizes procedural generation into six approaches. Her work bridges generative art, game development, and AI. She authored Chapter 1 "Getting Started with Generators" in Procedural Storytelling in Game Design.

## Key Contributions

- Created Tracery, the most widely used grammar-based generation library for Twitterbots and procedural text
- Identified the "10,000 Bowls of Oatmeal Problem" (mathematical uniqueness ≠ perceptual uniqueness)
- Developed the generative methods taxonomy (6 categories of procedural generation approaches)
- Pioneered the "artist-in-a-box" concept for generative systems
