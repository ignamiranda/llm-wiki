# My Experience Launching Eye of the Temple

## Summary
Postmortem of Eye of the Temple's PC VR launch: 96% positive Steam reviews but disappointing sales, analyzing causes including VR market size, demo cannibalization, and PR inexperience.

## Content

Eye of the Temple launched on PC VR to exceptional critical reception — 96% positive Steam reviews and strong acclaim from major VR outlets including UploadVR, Road to VR, and others. The game had built healthy pre-launch buzz through its innovative room-scale movement mechanic. Yet despite the positive reception, sales were mediocre, falling short of expectations.

The article identifies three key factors. First, the PC VR market in 2021 was still small — the install base of headsets capable of room-scale VR limited the addressable audience significantly. Second, the free demo may have cannibalized sales: developers are often encouraged to release demos for wishlisting and press coverage, but in this case, the demo may have satisfied players' curiosity without converting them to purchases. Third, PR inexperience meant that press coverage clustered around the demo release and did not carry through to the full launch — journalists covered the game once and did not return for the 1.0 release.

The lesson is that press outreach must be sustained through development, not concentrated at a single announcement. Mentions of Beardo Benjo, Naysy, Virtual Reality Oasis, and Nathie as community figures who covered the game. The article also references Mark Brown's GMTK video on mystery, connecting back to Rune's earlier design analysis work.

## Key Takeaways
- 96% positive reviews but mediocre sales
- Demo can cannibalize launch coverage
- PC VR market limitations in 2021
- Press outreach needs to be sustained, not just at launch
