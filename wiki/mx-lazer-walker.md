# Mx. Lazer-Walker

## Bio

Mx. Lazer-Walker is a researcher at the MIT Media Lab who created the Computational Flâneur, a site-specific generative poetry walk that combines neural net text generation with GPS-triggered real-world storytelling. Their work explores the intersection of digital procedural generation, physical environments, and embodied play — treating the real world as the canvas for generative narrative. They authored Chapter 17 "Generating in the Real World" in Procedural Storytelling in Game Design.

## Key Contributions

- Created the Computational Flâneur, a site-specific generative poetry walk
- Pioneered embodied procedural storytelling combining GPS, neural nets, and real-world environments
- Explored procedural generation as a site-specific, physically situated practice
