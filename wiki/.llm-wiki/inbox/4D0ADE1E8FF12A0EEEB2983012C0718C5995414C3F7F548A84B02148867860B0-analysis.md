## Source Summary / 来源摘要

Lima, Feijó & Furtado (2022) present a method combining automated planning with a genetic algorithm guided by story arcs to procedurally generate branching (nonlinear) quests for games. Quests are represented as tree structures where branches represent different story paths. The genetic algorithm uses a novel crossover operator based on state compatibility (equal/sub/super types), a fitness function comparing story arcs to a desired template, and semantic integrity constraints to maintain coherence. Evaluated via a Turing Test (48.68% accuracy — essentially indistinguishable from human-designed quests).
