# Oskar Stålberg

## Bio

Oskar Stålberg is an independent game developer best known for creating Townscaper (a town-building toy) and Bad North (a real-time tactics game). He is a pioneer in adapting the Wave Function Collapse algorithm to irregular grids, combining it with marching cubes for organic 3D geometry, and developing custom observation heuristics that avoid local minima. His work on Townscaper demonstrated WFC's potential for mixed-initiative design and became one of the most visible commercial applications of the algorithm.

## Key Contributions

- Adapted WFC to work on irregular (non-rectangular) grids
- Combined WFC with marching cubes for organic 3D geometry in Townscaper
- Developed a custom observation heuristic that doesn't produce local minima
- Applied WFC to level generation in Bad North with navigability-preserving heuristics
- Gave multiple talks about WFC and mixed-initiative generation at EPC, SGC, and AI and Games
