# Curated Narrative

## Definition

Tim Keenan and Benjamin Hill's approach in *Duskers*: static, hand-crafted narrative content is fragmented and reordered to coexist with procedural gameplay. The same narrative fragments can create different meanings depending on the order the player encounters them and the choices they make.

## Key Properties

- **Player story vs curated narrative** — The player's emergent story from gameplay runs alongside the curated narrative fragments; both coexist without one dominating
- **Order matters** — The same fragments encountered in different orders produce different interpretations and emotional arcs
- **Pillars of information** — Narrative content is organized around consistent thematic pillars that give coherence regardless of fragment order
- **Narrative as lure** — Curated objectives push the player into new procedural gameplay situations, using story as motivation to generate more emergent play

## Examples

In *Duskers*, the player finds logs, messages, and data fragments from previous crews. The order of discovery is procedural, but each fragment is hand-written. The player constructs the story of what happened from the fragments they find.
