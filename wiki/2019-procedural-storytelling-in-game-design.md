# Procedural Storytelling in Game Design

## Summary

This edited collection, published by CRC Press in 2019 and edited by Tanya X. Short and Tarn Adams, examines the evolving discipline of procedural storytelling in video games. Across 28 chapters by game industry practitioners, the book demonstrates how traditional storytelling tools — characterization, world-building, theme, momentum, atmosphere — can be adapted for procedurally generated narratives, using specific examples from their games.

## Content

The book is organized into 5 sections:

**Section 1: Introduction** — Foundational concepts including generator design, keeping procedural generation simple (via Spelunky case studies), adapting content to player choices, generating emotional impact, and ethical procedural generation.

**Section 2: Structure and Systems** — Narrative architecture techniques including the Ink narrative system (Weave Structure), curated narrative in Duskers, blending static and procedural fiction, dramatic play patterns in The Sims, story arcs in Curious Expedition, thematic amplification in Triple Town, emergent narrative in Dwarf Fortress, and heavily authored dynamic storytelling in Church in the Darkness.

**Section 3: Worlds and Context** — World-scale procedural storytelling including history generation in Caves of Qud, procedural descriptions in Voyageur, real-world procedural generation, dirty procedural narrative in We Happy Few, beyond-fun design in Frostpunk, and procedural storytelling in Dungeons & Dragons.

**Section 4: Characters** — Character-focused procedural techniques including maximizing impact of generated personalities, procedural characters in State of Decay 2, plot generators, personality generation in The Shrouded Isle, and procedural dialog systems.

**Section 5: Resources** — Practical tools and inspiration including tarot as procedural storytelling, Twitterbots, and creating authoring tools for procedural storytelling.

## Key Takeaways

- Procedural storytelling differs from pure procedural generation by prioritizing narrative coherence and emotional impact over content variety
- A spectrum exists between fully authored and fully procedural narratives, with most successful approaches blending both
- Character personality generation requires careful winnowing of contradictions to create believable characters
- World history generation provides narrative depth even in player-driven sandbox games
- Authoring tools are critical for making procedural storytelling accessible to non-programmer writers
