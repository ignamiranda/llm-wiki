# Intelligent Generation of Graphical Game Assets — A Systematic Review

## Summary

Fukaya, Daylamani-Zad & Agius (2023) present a systematic literature review of 200 papers on the procedural generation of graphical game assets — including clouds, buildings, vegetation, terrains, water, and textures. They propose a conceptual framework for procedural content generation (PCG) asset generation and taxonomize approaches by asset type, generation technique, and evaluation methodology.

## Content

The review covers:
- **Asset types**: Clouds, buildings, vegetation, terrain, water, textures
- **Generation techniques**: Rule-based systems, noise functions, L-systems, grammars, constraint satisfaction, machine learning
- **Evaluation methods**: Visual fidelity, performance, user studies, diversity metrics
- **Proposed framework**: A conceptual model decomposing PCG asset generation into asset specification, generation method selection, parameter tuning, and evaluation

The paper identifies key research gaps including limited cross-asset integration, insufficient evaluation standards, and under-explored AI-assisted generation approaches.

## Key Takeaways

- Broadest systematic review of graphical game asset PCG to date (200 papers)
- Proposes a standardized conceptual framework for describing and comparing PCG asset systems
- Identifies gaps that later work (Infinigen, ProcFunc) partially addresses
- Highlights lack of unified tooling and evaluation benchmarks
