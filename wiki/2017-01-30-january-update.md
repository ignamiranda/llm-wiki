# January Update: Visuals, Usability and Early Testing

## Summary

Visual improvements including gate models and stone torches, a redesigned intro section, a rolling block platform mechanic, and early tester signups on Itch.io for Eye of the Temple.

## Content

The January 2017 update marked a significant visual and usability pass on Eye of the Temple. Placeholder models were replaced with proper 3D models — notably, Rune taught himself modeling rather than contracting it out, since the specific VR interaction requirements made it faster to iterate in-house.

The intro section was redesigned to ease players in gradually. Originally the player started 4 meters above ground, which was intimidating; the new version started at a 0.75m drop, building confidence before introducing larger heights. A rolling block platform was developed as a bridge mechanism between spaces, serving as a gentler alternative to the rotating platforms that had been causing motion sickness in playtests.

Early tester signups were opened on Itch.io, beginning the external playtesting phase.

## Key Takeaways

- Learned 3D modeling instead of working with contractors
- Intro section starts at 0.75m drop instead of 4m
- Rolling block platform replaces rotating platforms that caused motion sickness
- Early tester signups on Itch.io
