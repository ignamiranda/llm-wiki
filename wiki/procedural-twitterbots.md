# Procedural Twitterbots

## Definition

Twitterbots are durational generative storytelling platforms that leverage the constraints of Twitter's platform — short message length, timeline context, profile metadata — to produce evocative narrative fragments over long periods. George Buckenham describes them as a form of procedural storytelling that treats the platform itself as part of the generative system.

## Key Properties

- **Worldbuilding through fragmentation** — Each post reveals a small piece of a larger fictional world; readers assemble the whole from fragments
- **Probability space curation** — The bot author curates what *can* happen, not what *does* happen; the audience experiences the actual output over time
- **Durational art** — Bots run for months or years, creating long-form narrative arcs through short bursts
- **Protest bots** — Bots used for political messaging, leveraging repurposed platform affordances
- **Broken bots as aesthetic objects** — When a platform API changes, a broken bot becomes a fossil of both the fiction and the platform

## Examples

Buckenham cites bots like @horse_ebooks (which was not a bot but inspired many), @everyword, and protest bots that repurpose Twitter's affordances for political ends. The broken state of bots after API changes is itself treated as meaningful.
