# Tarot of the Parrigues

## Definition

Tarot of the Parrigues is Emily Short's procedural storytelling game that uses tarot cards as its narrative generation mechanism. It adapts the associative, interpretive structure of tarot readings — where readers construct meaning from card positions and relationships — into an interactive fiction framework where players build narrative through symbolic card layouts.

## Key Properties

- Uses tarot card spreads as a narrative generation mechanism
- Players construct meaning from symbolic card positions and relationships
- Adapts the interpretive, associative logic of tarot readings to interactive fiction
- Each card carries multiple possible interpretations depending on context and position
- Embodies the principle that procedural storytelling can leverage existing interpretive frameworks

## Examples

In Tarot of the Parrigues, card positions in a spread determine narrative roles (e.g., protagonist, obstacle, resolution) while card meanings provide thematic content. Different card combinations generate different story configurations, much like how tarot readers derive unique readings from the same spread.
