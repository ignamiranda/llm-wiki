# Darius Kazemi

## Bio

Darius Kazemi is an independent game developer and creator known for his philosophy of starting procedural generation simple — what he calls "Brian Reynolds' rule" — and shipping quickly to learn from real-world feedback. He created influential Twitterbots including "Two Headlines" and developed the concept of banlists for ethical output filtering in procedural generation. He advocates for iterative, simple approaches over complex initial designs. He authored Chapter 2 "Keeping Procedural Generation Simple" in Procedural Storytelling in Game Design.

## Key Contributions

- Articulated "Brian Reynolds' rule": start with the simplest possible generator and iterate
- Developed banlists as an ethical filtering mechanism for procedural output
- Created influential Twitterbots including "Two Headlines" and "Mars Agriculture"
