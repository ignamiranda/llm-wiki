# Joseph Campbell

## Bio
Joseph Campbell (1904–1987) was an American comparative mythologist, professor, and author. His seminal work, *The Hero with a Thousand Faces* (1949), introduced the concept of the [[monomyth]] — a universal narrative pattern found across world mythologies, religions, and stories. Campbell's monomyth describes a hero's journey through stages including the Call to Adventure, Crossing the Threshold, Road of Trials, Meeting with the Goddess, Atonement with the Father, Apotheosis, Magic Flight, and Return with the Elixir. His work has profoundly influenced screenwriting, most notably through [[dan-harmon]]'s [[harmon-story-circle]], which maps Campbell's stages onto an 8-step circular model.

## Key Contributions
- *The Hero with a Thousand Faces* (1949) — introduced the [[monomyth]] concept
- The four functions of myth: mystical, cosmological, sociological, pedagogical
- His work directly inspired [[dan-harmon]]'s [[harmon-story-circle]] and influenced countless filmmakers (George Lucas, Christopher Vogler)
