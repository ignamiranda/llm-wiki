# One Year Later

## Summary

One year after the launch of Eye of the Temple, the game received post-launch updates including save slots and speedrun challenges. Sales covered roughly two-thirds of development costs. A Quest 2 port was underway, and the next project — The Big Forest — was publicly teased for the first time.

## Content

The first year after Eye of the Temple's launch saw several quality-of-life updates including save slots for managing multiple playthroughs and speedrun challenges for replayability. Financially, the game's sales covered approximately two-thirds of its development costs, a reasonable outcome for a niche VR title but short of full recoupment.

Efforts toward a Quest 2 port were underway in collaboration with Salmi Games, recognizing the standalone headset's larger installed base. The Big Forest was teased as the next project, signaling a return to the procedural generation strengths of earlier work, now applied to a first-person exploration context.

## Key Takeaways

- Eye of the Temple sales covered ~2/3 of development costs
- The Big Forest teased as next project
