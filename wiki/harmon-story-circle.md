# Harmon Story Circle

## Definition
The Harmon Story Circle is an 8-step circular narrative model developed by [[dan-harmon]] for [[channel-101]]'s short film format. It describes story as a descent into the unknown and eventual return, with 8 numbered positions arranged clockwise around a circle divided into four quadrants (top = ordinary world, bottom = special world). The model synthesizes [[joseph-campbell]]'s [[monomyth]] (Hero's Journey) with observations about biological, psychological, and social rhythms.

## The 8 Steps

1. **You** — A character is in a zone of comfort (establish protagonist)
2. **Need** — But they want something; something is off balance (call to adventure)
3. **Go** — They enter an unfamiliar situation (crossing the threshold)
4. **Search** — Adapt to it, get broken down (road of trials)
5. **Find** — Get what they wanted; major revelation (meeting with the goddess / midpoint)
6. **Take** — Pay a heavy price for it (atonement with the father / plot point 2)
7. **Return** — Return to their familiar situation (magic flight / return threshold)
8. **Change** — Having changed (master of both worlds)

## Key Properties
- Circular: the protagonist ends where they started but fundamentally transformed
- Symmetric: steps 1 ↔ 5 (maternal/vulnerable), 2 ↔ 6 (paternal/active), 3 ↔ 7 (thresholds), 4 ↔ 8 (competence)
- Universal: Harmon argues the pattern is hardwired into human instinct and recognizable across cultures
- Recursive: each step can itself contain a full mini-circle of 8 sub-steps
- Variable: the circle can be "hand drawn" — precision matters less than instinctive resonance

## Examples
- **Die Hard** (analyzed in article 104): John McClane on plane (1) → rocky marriage (2) → terrorists attack party (3) → learns ropes, loses shoes (4) → realizes love for wife (5) → battles blonde terrorist (6) → leaps off building (7) → saves wife, kills Hans (8)
- **The Matrix**: Neo as office worker (1) → weird call (2) → reality is illusion (3) → learns kung fu (4) → talks to Oracle (5) → loses Morpheus (6) → goes back (7) → saves the day (8)
