# Monomyth

## Definition
The monomyth, also known as the Hero's Journey, is a universal narrative pattern identified by [[joseph-campbell]] in *The Hero with a Thousand Faces* (1949). It describes a hero who ventures forth from the ordinary world into a supernatural region, faces trials and a supreme ordeal, and returns with the power to bestow boons on their community. [[dan-harmon]] mapped the monomyth's stages onto his 8-step [[harmon-story-circle]].

## Key Properties
- Universal across cultures, religions, and time periods
- Describes both external adventure and internal psychological transformation
- Key stages: Call to Adventure, Crossing the Threshold, Road of Trials, Meeting with the Goddess, Atonement with the Father, Apotheosis, Magic Flight, Return with the Elixir
- Directly inspired the [[harmon-story-circle]] and Christopher Vogler's *The Writer's Journey*
