# Historical Rationalization

## Definition

Historical rationalization, developed by Jason Grinblat for *Caves of Qud* (Chapter 15), is a procedural history generation technique where *effect precedes cause*. Events occur, a "gospel" is generated to explain them, and that gospel fabricates a reason retroactively. The system intentionally encodes its own ignorance about its world history — producing myth rather than chronicle.

## Key Properties

- **Historical entities vs events**: entities (factions, creatures, sultans) exist first; events emerge from their interactions
- **Gospels**: discrete text units that describe an event with deliberate narrative bias and interpretative gaps
- **Sultan domains**: archetypal epithets that associate historical figures with domains (e.g., "Sultan of Rust"), creating mythic resonance
- **Apophenia as design tool**: the system exploits the player's tendency to over-interpret patterns, making fragmentary histories feel richer than authored ones

## Examples

- **Caves of Qud** — procedurally generated sultan histories with conflicting gospel accounts, holy sites that reference events the player can never verify, and faction histories that contradict each other
- **Dwarf Fortress** — legends mode provides similar retroactively-generated historical narratives with deliberate gaps
