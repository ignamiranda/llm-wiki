# Dirty Narrative

## Definition

Dirty narrative, articulated by Alex Epstein in the context of *We Happy Few* (Chapter 18), is a storytelling approach that intentionally fragments narrative information and requires active player interpretation. Instead of delivering a clean, complete story, the system presents gaps, ambiguities, inconsistencies, and absences that the player must resolve through inference and engagement.

## Key Properties

- **Five techniques**: translucent lies, absences, mysteries, inconsistencies, and tangents — each creates a different type of narrative gap
- **Pull vs push storytelling**: dirty narrative pulls players to investigate rather than pushing exposition at them
- **Multivalent story elements**: pieces of information that support multiple contradictory interpretations simultaneously
- **Cognitive fallacy-driven engagement**: leverages the human tendency to see patterns and fill gaps (apophenia) to make the player an active co-author

## Examples

- **We Happy Few** — fragmentary notes, unreliable NPC testimony, and contradictory environmental storytelling that forces players to construct their own understanding of Wellington Wells
- **Dwarf Fortress** — legends mode provides incomplete historical records that players must interpret
- **Dark Souls** — item descriptions and sparse NPC dialog create a lore that is pieced together by the community
