# Information Markets

## Definition

A proposed economic layer where AI agents purchase data from physical-world sensors, humans, and other agents. As AI systems become more capable, they exhaust already-digital information and need fresh data from the physical world. Information markets enable agents to acquire this data by offering prices for specific observations — a photo from a location, a lab experiment result, a market observation.

## Key Properties

- Bridge between digital AI and physical world
- Price signals determine which data gets collected
- Humans and IoT devices serve as sensors
- Agents bid for data to improve predictions and decisions
- Enables closed-loop intelligence that reaches beyond digital

## Examples

An AI agent trying to predict a geopolitical event could pay $10 for a photo or video from a specific location. No human reviews the data — agents process it directly. This extends the agentic web into physical observation.
