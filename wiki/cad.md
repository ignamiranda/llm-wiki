# Computer-Aided Design (CAD)

## Definition

Computer-Aided Design (CAD) is the use of software to create, modify, analyze, and document designs for manufacturing, engineering, architecture, and related fields. CAD systems range from parametric solid modelers (Fusion 360, SolidWorks) to script-based tools (OpenSCAD) and functional representation frameworks (libfive), each offering different tradeoffs between precision, automation, and ease of use.

## Types of CAD

- **Parametric CAD** — dimension-driven feature-based modeling (SolidWorks, Fusion 360)
- **Script-based CAD** — code-defined geometry ([[OpenSCAD]])
- **Functional CAD** — mathematical function-based modeling ([[libfive]], F-Rep)
- **Mesh-based CAD** — direct mesh editing and sculpting (Blender)
